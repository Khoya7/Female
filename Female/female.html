Khasati

Can a spark of pure, human connection, kindled by art, survive the relentless pressure of societal and religious divides? Khasati is a powerful, non-fictional novel set in Northern Nigeria. It follows Hallittah, a Christian, and Saleem, a Muslim—two ambitious professionals whose only common ground is a deep, shared passion for the 13th-century Sufi poet Rumi, turns into something Romantic. The novel Khasati sheds light on the beautiful northern Nigerian culture, and the complex ties between love and Religion.

About Her

A Northern Female is the powerful pen name of Deborah Bingbeng John, a distinguished writer, poet, and youth advocate from Taraba State, Nigeria. She embodies this identity, using it to narrate how her upbringing in Northern Nigeria shaped her, portraying the region through a lens of beautiful cultural bursts and deep tradition.

She currently serves as the Special Adviser on Education to the Governor of Taraba State. Her debut non-fiction book, Cold the Warmth is Inside, tells her life story, weaving her personal experiences as a young northern Nigerian woman with a global perspective. Beyond her writing and government role, she is the founder of the Future Ready Youth Initiative and the Northern Female Foundation, both dedicated to positively impacting and empowering Nigerian Youth.
